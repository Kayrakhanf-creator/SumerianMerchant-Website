Questions

Asked at the counter.

If your question isn’t here, ask on Discord — the door is open.

What is Sumerian Merchant? +
A first-person merchant simulator set in Mesopotamia around 2000 BCE. You run a state-appointed shop: stock shelves, weigh barley payments on a real balance scale, negotiate, take pawns, file official complaints, pay taxes, and rise through society.
When can I play it? +
A free demo is planned for Steam Next Fest in October 2026 — roughly your first ten days in the city. Wishlist the game on Steam to be notified the moment it's live.
What platforms? +
PC via Steam first (Windows). Other platforms may follow after 1.0 — nothing to announce yet.
How much will it cost? +
Not final. We'll announce pricing closer to Early Access.
Is it historically accurate? +
Grounded, not academic. Barley as everyday money, weighed silver for large deals, state-regulated prices, clay-tablet complaints, cylinder seals — all real. Then we take deliberate liberties where play demands it. Ea-nāṣir's famous copper complaints date to around 1750 BCE, a few centuries after our setting. We moved him. He'd have moved himself for a good margin.
Is there combat? +
No. Your weapons are a scale, a stylus, and a reputation.
Single-player? Co-op? +
Single-player first. Co-op is something we want to explore after 1.0.
What about the strange rumors? +
The city keeps records. Some of them shouldn't exist. That's all we'll say.
What engine is it built in? +
Unity.
What languages will it support? +
English at launch, with Turkish and other languages planned for the full release.
