02 Signature mechanic — try it

Payment has weight.

No coins. No floating numbers. The stones sit on one pan — you pour barley into the other until the beam believes you.

Hold the sack. Pour.

In game, your weight set grows with your standing — 1 · 2 · 5 · 10 · 25 · 50, and up.

03 The work of a merchant

One counter. A whole life.

03 · 1The daily loop

Every morning begins the same.

  1. Open shop
  2. Order goods
  3. Stock shelves
  4. Bargain
  5. Weigh barley
  6. Handle complaints
  7. Pay tax
  8. Close the day

Then a new day begins — and the city remembers yesterday.

03 · 2Progression

Influence is progression.

Money keeps the shop open. Influence opens the city.

Ledger of influence 𒀭

  • Better customers New faces trust the counter
  • Rarer goods Shelf space earns its keep
  • Special orders Sealed requests, named buyers
  • New districts Doors open past the market
  • Higher society The city looks at you differently

03 · 3Buying & inspection

Turn it over in your hands.

People bring you things — copper, seals, statues, a dead man’s tools. Weigh the piece. Read the surface. Name your price and defend it.

Early on, the truth is plain. Later, fakes learn to hide.

03 · 4Pawn & collateral

Some customers do not come to buy.

They come to survive. Accept collateral, set terms, wait. If they return, it’s theirs. If they don’t, it’s yours.
