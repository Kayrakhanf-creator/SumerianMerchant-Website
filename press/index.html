Description

Sumerian Merchant is a first-person merchant simulator set in Mesopotamia around 2000 BCE. As a state-appointed trader, you run a shop at the heart of one of the world’s first cities: order goods at state prices, stock your shelves, and serve a street of customers who each want something different from you.

Its signature mechanic is the balance scale. There are no coins and no floating numbers — barley is money, and every payment is weighed by hand against stone weights. Silver, for the deals that matter, is weighed too.

Around the scale sits a society. A pawn counter for citizens in hard times. An official complaint system — press your case into clay and accept the judgment, knowing the same law can be turned on you. A 28-day year of seasons, festivals, droughts, caravans, and war. Seven social classes between you and the royal house, unlocked not by experience points but by influence.

And beneath the routine, something else. The city keeps records. Not all of them should exist.

Key features

  • Weigh every payment on a physical balance scale — barley in, trust out.
  • Rise through seven social classes; influence, not XP, is progression.
  • File official complaints on clay tablets — and answer the ones filed against you.
  • Run a pawn counter with state-set interest and human stakes.
  • Trade through a living calendar: drought, harvest, festival, disease, caravans, war.
  • Inspect goods for quality and forgery before the inspector inspects you.
  • Collect real historical artifacts and complete regional collections.
  • Meet Ea-nāṣir, history’s most complained-about copper merchant.
